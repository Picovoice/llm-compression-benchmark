A glass is partially filled with water. Five ice cubes are placed in the glass, causing the level of the water to reach the rim of the glass. Which of the following statements best explains the increase in water level? (A) The volume of the submerged ice is equal to the volume of water displaced. (B) The mass of the water in the glass is less than the mass of the ice. (C) The weight of the ice is less than the weight of the water in the glass. (D) The density of the water in the glass is greater than the density of the ice.
Answer: A
