In an effort to preserve wildlife on his farm in Massachusetts, a farmer decides to stop using a 10-acre field. The farmer fences off the area, stops cutting the grass, and stops allowing livestock to graze on it. After twenty years, the area would most likely (A) be covered with moss and rocks. (B) be a mature hardwood forest. (C) be grown over with bushes and small trees. (D) be barren due to lack of maintenance.
Answer: C
